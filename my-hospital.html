My Hospital (iOS & Android)

Game Producer · F2P Simulation · Concept → Launch → LiveOps

Aug 2019 – Jul 2024 · Built team 0→12 · Monthly releases · Weekly/seasonal events

Snapshot

  • Role: Game Producer
  • Timeframe: Aug 2019 – Jul 2024
  • Team: 4 Unity devs · 4 graphic designers · 1 game designer · 2 QA · 3 backend devs
  • External: performance marketing team (static + video ads for Meta & Google)
  • Scope: concept → global launch → LiveOps

Product outcomes

  • 15M+ downloads
  • $10M+ revenue
  • 15K DAU
  • ~40% D1 retention
  • $2.7 ARPDAU
  • +43% conversion uplift
  • LiveOps cadence: weekly & monthly recurring events + monthly seasonal custom events

The challenge

  • Build and scale a team from scratch (0 → 12).
  • Ship and operate a competitive F2P simulation with predictable monthly releases.
  • Increase monetisation performance while preserving player trust.
  • Migrate backend infrastructure without disrupting LiveOps.
  • Resolve performance issues introduced by major content expansion.

My responsibilities

  • Milestone planning & roadmap definition
  • Backlog prioritization & sprint planning
  • Cross-discipline coordination (dev, art, design, QA, backend)
  • Release planning & store submissions
  • LiveOps planning and cadence
  • Stakeholder communication
  • Performance marketing coordination
  • Infrastructure migration planning

Key initiatives & impact

1) Team scaling (0 → 12)

  • Recruited the full team and built a culture aligned with the simulation genre and F2P economics.
  • Established production rituals for predictable monthly delivery.
  • Built a team aligned with product goals: retention, conversion, and sustainable LiveOps value.
  • Aligned everyone on the “honest value exchange”: players pay when they receive quality content.

2) LiveOps system & content expansion

  • Established recurring LiveOps cadence (weekly/monthly) and monthly seasonal custom events.
  • Doubled the amount of content through coordinated pipelines and release planning.

3) Monetisation redesign (dynamic pricing)

Problem: High-DAU regions were under-monetising with a one-size-fits-all model.

  • Implemented a dynamic pricing structure based on player demographics, region, and usage.
  • Enabled fine-tuning of price points and monetisation mix per segment.

Result: +43% conversion uplift.

4) Tech debt: backend migration

  • Migrated from serverless DynamoDB to a fully supported backend (Node.js + document DB).
  • Created a phased migration plan integrated into the roadmap to reduce risk.

5) Performance issues after content expansion

  • Resolved performance issues caused by increased game size by redesigning the game architecture.
  • Balanced optimization work with ongoing feature delivery.

6) Tight monthly releases

  • Coordinated development + QA to maintain monthly releases while addressing QA-reported defects.
  • Solved through prioritization discipline and strong scope control.

Learnings

  • F2P success requires cultural alignment between fun, value, and monetisation.
  • Infrastructure planning is as critical as feature planning.
  • Content scale must be supported by architecture.
  • Predictable cadence creates stability and better decision-making.
